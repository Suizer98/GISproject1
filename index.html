Before we go to research topic,
let's us differentiate between GIS and Geomatics first.

To have a better understanding of them, we need to look how the terms originate. By looking at the figure on the right side, we can already understand the barrier between.

> A geographic information system (GIS) is a computer system that can analyse and map almost anything, whether it's moving or fixed. A GIS combines standard database activities like query and statistical analysis with the ability to see data in place and time. GIS-created maps are valuable for displaying locations and events that occur there, such as disease outbreaks. They can be used to analyse and visualise any spatial or spatio-temporal (time-varying) system, such as mapping a moving tectonic plates, to displaying a which parking place is occupied.

> In fact, GIS is earlier mentioned than Geomatics by Dr. Roger Tomlinson in 1960s. It was used to define the Canadian Geographic Information System. The GIS system used a layer-based approach to map management. Eventually, GIS has evolved into a computer-based tool for storing and processing map-based land data since that time.

> Geomatics is a relatively new term created by Michel Paradis in 1980s as a unified framework for today's multidisciplinary issues related to Earth's space. Geomatics' goal was to master specific technology and spatial data collection methods. Furthermore, the focus is on the synergies that can be achieved by merging digital technology from many fields.

> Paradis used the term Geomatics to describe how modern technological data capture was becoming more ubiquitous in disciplines like surveying, photogrammetry, geodesy, and hydrography. From data collection to distribution, it covered a wide range of methods and technologies.

The research topic to be discussed:
Electric Vehicle Charging Stations Coverage: A Study of Slovenia

Website URL: Click here
Researcher's name: Klemen PRAH*, Mark KMETEC, Matjaž KNEZ
Researcher's institution: University of Maribor

The reason of this study

Insufficient electric infrastructure is said to have contributed to the slow development of Electric Vehicle, the first mass-produced and purpose-designed electric car of the modern age. A good deployment of EVCSs is thus one of the most important solutions for more efficient and widespread use of electric cars.

As the rapid growth of new and creative modes of passenger transportation, the researcher pay more attention on the location intelligence, that can be managed by using geographic information systems, that is more crucial than ever

The overall goal of the research is to make it easier to make decisions about how to expand, deploy, and improve EVCS infrastructure.

The time period and
the data involved

This research is published on year 2022. The data is updated to the latest acquired from the website http://polni.si/ which contain every EVs charging stations in Slovenia.

For the data involved, as mentioned above, all data was further created in the shapefile format as digital vector layers. The data contained geolocation data of electric vehicle charging stations to be imported into Arcgis software.

To further support the dataset, the researchers performed a online qualitative survey to complete the coverage of EV stations with 22 questions. The number of respondents was 25, with all of them owners of electric cars.

Spatial extends of this study and
the software that was used

The spatial extends although not mentioned by the researchers in numerical ways, but we know that it involved all area in Slovenia country. So, the area covered is 20,271 square kilometres equivalent to the domain of SLovenia. The latitude and longitude of the capital of Slovenia, which is called Ljubljana located around 46° 03'North and 14° 31' East.
